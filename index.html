🚨 YOU'VE BEEN PULLED OVER
1
Stay calm — pull over safely
Switch on your hazard lights, slow down gradually and pull to the left side of the road. Do not argue or try to drive away.
2
Keep your documents ready
Have these ready to show the officer:
📄 Driving Licence (DL)
📋 Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC)
🛡️ Insurance Certificate (valid)
💨 Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate
3
Know your rights
You have the legal right to:
✅ Receive a printed or e-challan receipt
✅ Know the exact section of law violated
✅ Pay the challan online — not cash on the spot
✅ Contest the challan in court if you disagree
4
Do NOT pay cash on the spot
Paying cash without a receipt is illegal. Officers must issue an official e-challan. Demand a receipt for any payment.
5
Pay or contest online
Once you receive your challan number:
💻 Pay online: echallan.parivahan.gov.in
📱 Or use the mParivahan app
⚖️ To contest: visit your local RTO or magistrate court within 60 days
6
Emergency contacts
If you feel unsafe or the officer is behaving inappropriately:
🚔 Police helpline: 100
🆘 Emergency (all services): 112
👮 Traffic police complaint: contact your state traffic police website
